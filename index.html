8 honest reasons I'm applying
01
Personal brands are the part of content I keep choosing, even when I don't have to

I've done SEO, case studies, sales enablement, comparison pages, newsletter strategy, outbound copy. I can do all of it. But the work I find myself doing on weekends, taking on as side clients, thinking about when I'm not supposed to be working, it's always personal brands.

The reason, I think, is that it's the only format where you can't fake it. It only works when there's something real behind it. Finding that thing, shaping it, getting a founder to say what they actually think instead of the safe version, that's the interesting problem.

02
I built the LinkedIn presence of a CEO whose company was acquired by Microsoft
Still have the cap.

At Minit, working with James Dening (CEO) on his personal brand was a core part of my content role. Biweekly recorded sessions. Me preparing questions, shaping strategy, writing and scheduling posts, coordinating with design, researching what to comment on. What worked on LinkedIn fed into the newsletter. The newsletter fed back into LinkedIn.

Minit was acquired by Microsoft in 2022, one of the more significant tech exits in Slovakia. One moment that stuck: James mentioned in a meeting that he'd reached out to a team member going through something difficult — not about work, just as a person. I suggested we put that story in the newsletter. He hadn't thought about it that way. The response was unlike anything we'd sent before. People wrote back to say it was exactly what they needed to read that week. That's the version of a founder's voice that actually builds trust.

"Michal has been critical in driving Minit's growth and success during the three years we worked together until the company was acquired by Microsoft, one of (if not) the most successful exits in Slovakia." Daniel Tomcala, VP Marketing at TurnKey Lender, previously managed Michal directly at Minit
03
Five exec brands across process mining, fintech, solar, and private equity

James Dening at Minit. Rasto Hlavac, Minit's original founder, now an investor (Forbes Slovakia cover, 2023). Dmytro Voronenko, founder of TurnKey Lender. Igor Strecko, former founder and CEO of Webglobe, now managing partner at Neozone Collective. Marcel Suri, founder and CEO of Solargis.

Each one is different — in their relationship to being public, in how much they trust the process, in what they actually want to say versus what their audience needs to hear. That gap between the two is usually where the real work happens. Managing multiple accounts simultaneously while navigating those differences is something I do with AI as a core part of the process — usually Claude — for drafts, structure, and iteration. The judgment about what's true to the person and what's worth saying, that part stays human.

04
At Solargis, content finally reflected what the company actually did

Working with the head of marketing and a designer, we overhauled the website, built content guidelines, and developed a LinkedIn strategy. Company page followers grew by 33% in the first year and have been growing steadily since. More importantly, the content finally said something worth reading — solar energy data and analytics used by developers, grid operators, and financial institutions worldwide.

Writing for that audience means understanding that the buyer is a project finance engineer who will fact-check everything you say. It changed how I think about what accuracy means in B2B content.

05
When I can't find the right tool, I build it

At TurnKey Lender I built a ghostwriting assistant for the sales team. We fed it their previous posts, call transcripts, message histories. Built a detailed prompt to identify their voice. Wrote hard rules for what it should never do: mirrored contrast statements, em dashes, tagline-style endings. Gave it a framework for post length. The goal was a starting point that sounded like them, so they would use it.

I've also built: a speed reader, a HIIT timer, a personal website, and this page. AI is a heavy part of how I work — usually Claude. First drafts, thinking partner, build partner when I need something that doesn't exist yet.

06
I trained sales teams to show up on LinkedIn like people, not accounts

At TurnKey Lender and Solargis I ran LinkedIn training for sales and marketing teams: how to find your niche, how to build around the 3 Cs (connection, commenting, content), how to write posts that provide value before asking for anything. Then built the AI ghostwriter so they could actually keep going after the training ended.

The challenge is always the same. They want to post. They sit down. Nothing comes out. Or what comes out sounds like a press release. The system I built was designed to get them past that point without doing the thinking for them.

07
I started in editorial work before moving into B2B

Before B2B SaaS, I ran a media platform covering the Slovak startup ecosystem. Interviews, profiles, features. Later I worked on the closing report of the Slovak-American Foundation, mapping 13 years of their work in Slovakia.

That kind of work is where I learned to find the angle that makes someone want to read something, not just communicate information. Give the reader a reason to care before you give them the facts. I also run a small podcast about fatherhood in Slovakia. Nothing to do with B2B. That is exactly why I do it.

08
Every exec is different — and that's the actual challenge

Some clients know exactly what they want to say and need help shaping it for an audience. Others need more of a thought partner — someone to figure out together what's actually worth sharing. Marcel took two years of conversations before he believed he had something worth saying publicly. Igor needed to shape a coherent narrative across a company acquisition, a personal transition, and a new chapter as an investor — all at once, without it feeling like a PR exercise. Dmytro needed to show up as a fintech authority in front of bank buyers who would fact-check everything.

Each one required a different approach. Same platform, same tools, completely different human being on the other side. Some of these are ongoing. Some were in the past. That's the part of this work nobody talks about enough — and the part I find most interesting.
